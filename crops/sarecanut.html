Introdution of Arecanut

The areca nut is the fruit of the areca palm, which grows in much of the tropical Pacific, South Asia, Southeast Asia, and parts of east Africa. It is commonly referred to as betel nut, not to be confused with betel leaves that are often used to wrap it.The areca nut is not a true nut, but rather the seed of a fruit categorized as a berry. It is commercially available in dried, cured, and fresh forms.Arecanut or betlelnut is an extensively cultivated tropical palm. Climate and Soil Requirement for Arecanut

The largest area under the crop is found in gravelly laterite soils of red clay type. It can also be grown on fertile clay loam soils. Sticky clay, sandy, alluvial, brackish and calcareous soils are not suitable for arecanut cultivation.Arecanut is capable of growing in a variety of soils. It thrives best in well drained soils. Adequate protection from exposure to South-Western sun is essential to avoid sun-scorch. Quick growing shade trees have to be planted on the southern and western sides well in advance of planting seedlings.

Water Supply for Arecanut

Irrigate weekly once during November to February, once in 4 days during March to May. Flood irrigation 175 lit/ tree/ day. In drip irrigation 16 to 20 lit/ tree/ day. Apply to each bearing palm (5 years and above) 10 - 15 kg of FYM or green leaf.

Harvestingn of Arecanut

The bearing starts after 5 years of planting. Nuts are harvested when they are three quarters ripe. The number of harvests will vary from three to five in one year depending upon the season and place of cultivation.
