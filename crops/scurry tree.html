CURRY PLANT

Introdution of Curry Plant

Curry leaves are a part of a small deciduous aromatic shrub, scientifically named Murraya koenigii, belonging to the family Rutaceae. It may be considered a natural medicinal plant. South Asia is home to this plant, and it is found in countries like Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, China and India. The history of curry leaves are seen in early 1st to 4th century AD. In Tamil and Kannada literature it was updated as word 'kari' with its uses. The word now popularly used for the Murraya koenigii is curry leaf which is originated from Tamil word Kari which means as 'spiced sauce'. February to March is the best time to sow the plant. Curry leaf plants are grown from both seeds and cuttings.

Climate and Soil Requirement for Curry Plant

The curry leaf plant needs well-drained soil mixed with cow dung or organic compost and river sand. The Curry Leaf Plant likes slightly acidic (Soil PH between 5.6 to 6 ), loose, and well-drained soil. After watering, the access water should run away from the drainage hole below the pot to prevent roots from rotting. Any kind of potting mix available in the big box stores will work for the curry leaf plant.

Water Supply for Curry Plant

The curry tree prefers dry soil. Water. Water regularly for the first two months after planting and allow the soil to dry out after a heavy rain.Watering. Never overwater your curry leaf plant as that kills them really fast. In the summer, water the plant every 2-3 days and keep tabs on small mites. In the fall season, watering is needed only on weekly basis.
