Information

Champa, also known as campaka in Sanskrit, is a big, evergreen tree with long, thin petals and fragrant blossoms. The blossoms come in a variety of colors, ranging from creamy white to orange. The Champa flower has a strong and distinct aroma, which has made it an essential aspect of Indian spiritual worship since time immemorial. It’s a thin-barked deciduous tree. The stem is shaped like a sausage and is grey-green in color. Because it is deciduous, the leaves fall away throughout the winter season, leaving only the stem. Here is some information and photos about harvesting champa flowers: When to harvest Champa flowers typically bloom from spring to fall, with peak blooming periods varying depending on the climate. It is best to harvest champa flowers early in the morning or late in the afternoon, as the flowers are at their most fragrant during these times. harvest Champa flowers are typically harvested by hand, using sharp scissors or pruning shears. Storing champa flowers: Champa flowers should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. To keep the flowers fresh for longer, you can place them in a vase with fresh water or wrap them in damp paper towels and store them in a plastic bag in the refrigerator.
