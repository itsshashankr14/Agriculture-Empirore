BOTTLE GOURD

Introdution of Bottle Gourd

The bottle gourd is a vigorous, annual, running or climbing vine with large leaves and a lush appearance. It grows fast and may begin to flower only 2 months after seeding. The thick stem is furrowed longitudinally. The vine is branched and climbs by means of tendrils along the stem.Bottle gourd vines are quick-growing annuals with hairy stems, long forked tendrils, and a musky odour. Many forms of the bottle gourd have been cultivated for specific purposes, and the sizes of the vines, leaves, and flowers, as well as the sizes and shapes of the fruits, vary greatly.

Climate and Soil Requirement for Bottle Gourd

Bottle gourd crop can be grown thrice a year, Zaid, Kharif and Rabi season. Zaid season crop can be grown from mid-January to February, Kharif season crop can be grown from mid-June to July & Rabi season crop can be grown from mid-September to October. Bottle gourd requires hot and humid climate. This crop is very sensitive to frost & it does not tolerate frost. So it is very necessary that there should be no frost for 4 months for is better growth. Temperature of about 20 to 32°C is suitable for its cultivation. Bottle gourd can be cultivated in different types of soil but it is considered that light loamy soil that is rich in organic matter with a good water holding capacity having a pH value of 6-7 is suitable for its cultivation. There should also be proper drainage system in the field.

Water Supply for Bottle Gourd

Drip irrigation is most advantageous in the bottle gourd cultivation. The summer crop requires frequent watering at an interval of 3 to 4 days. The winter crop is irrigated as and when needed. Usually, the rainy season crop does not require irrigation. Install drip system with main and sub-main pipes and place the inline lateral tubes at an interval of 1.5m. Place the drippers at an interval of 60 cm and 50 cm spacing with 4LPH and 3.5LPH capacities respectively.

Harvestingn of Bottle Gourd

Bottle gourd may be harvested 55-75 days after sowing. The fruit should be harvested when the rind of the fruit is very tender and green.Bottle gourds are permitted to obtain a maximum maturity on the vine before harvest. Harvest gourds when vines are dry. Fruits that are not fully mature or that have been injured do not store well. The fruits are ready for harvest 60-120 days after sowing depending on the variety.Harvesting. he crop of bitter gourd takes about 55-60 days from seed sowing to reach first harvest. Further pickings should be done at an interval of 2-3 days as bitter gourd fruits mature very fast and turn red.

bottle gourd, (Lagenaria siceraria), also called white-flowered gourd or calabash gourd, running or climbing vine of the gourd family (Cucurbitaceae), native to tropical Africa but cultivated in warm climates around the world for its ornamental and useful hard-shelled fruits.
